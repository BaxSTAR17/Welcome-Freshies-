What programming languages will we learn?

For your freshman year, You will be learning web development for your major subject, CCS 1 and that includes languages like HTML, CSS, JavaScript and also some PHP. You will also be learning the C programming language for your other major subject, CCS 2.

It's all Math?

Short Answer is No. Long Answer is, while a lot of concepts of math is involved in this field, it will only serve as an asset and a tool to a much more needed skill and concept for computer studies and that is Problem Solving. So knowledge and proficiency in math CAN be helpful, but it is not what this college revolves around.

Should I use ChatGPT?

ChatGPT must only be treated as a tool to further learn, it is not recommended to use it for automatic and handicapped labor. Especially in asking for ChatGPT to generate code, which is still very much susceptible and vulnerable to bugs.

Do I need a laptop?

No. You'll do all the programming, coding and activities inside the laboratories which has tons of computers. Laptops are only useful for being able to do your projects and activities anywhere else than your home.

Do we write code on paper?

Yes but most of the time, this only happens during tests, not necessarily activities or projects, everything else is to be typed and encoded.

Are we gonna make games?

Sometimes, and while there is a Game Development Elective Track for BSIT students in the future years, The college will not always focus on game development and will branch out more onto the technical and computing side of things.

Yes, especially during your CCS 1 and GE10 subject

ABOUT GE10
GE10 (General Education 10) Better known as "Whole Person Education" is a mandatory minor subject taken by every course wherein it aims to develop you to be a true sillimanian as a whole, mentally, physically and spiritually through teaching its history, extracurricular activities, campus tours and etc. GE10 is usually unique for every college, but the gist of the subject is still maintained. For the College of Computer Studies, The Final Project is a bit more special.
You will be tasked to make an Online E-Journal! It is basically a website that'll hold journal entries/reflections! Yes, you'll be making your own website, it may sound difficult, but don't worry, You're allowed to use drag and drop site makers like wix and google sites. You'll also be taking CCS 1 as one of your majors which tackles web development so it can help with this project.
Your website will hold journal reflections of your experiences in attending a series of events that correlate to Silliman's 5 C's, Church, Classroom, Culture, Community and Court ONLY during the first semester. Each C requires 8 points, and all respective events you attend for the 5 C's have a certain number of points depending on your participation, It scales from 1 pt. which means you were just part of the audience for the event, into 4pts. which means you played a very important role in the event, especially if you're the lead performer.
It is required that you must have 8 pts. per C, and that the events you attend must go under the correct C in your website. Church require religious activities, Classroom require lectures, speeches, and workshops, Culture require Cultural events, or important traditional events, Community require reachouts and service events, and Court require sports events. Journal Reflections per event you attended are also required in your website as they make up the content of your site and they also have a format (to be provided) to be followed.
So far, That's all what you need to know for GE10 for now !
